# Mobile Reference Visual Rules

## What AMS may borrow

- Mobile-first single-column hierarchy with a fast first screenful.
- Four stable primary destinations in the bottom navigation.
- Reusable card families for feeds, settings groups, and directory-style lists.
- Compact profile and settings sections broken into smaller chunks.
- Clear feed-item hierarchy: priority, title, context, action.
- Generous tap targets, spacing, and safe-area-aware mobile chrome.
- Simple help/legal patterns based on lists and accordions instead of dense mixed layouts.

## What AMS must not copy

- Exact screen compositions, iconography, or card outlines from the reference.
- The reference palette, washed pink/white tonality, or photography treatment.
- The reference app's copy, screen names, ordering, or Hebrew wording.
- Exact grid counts or destination ordering on the home screen.
- The exact icon-in-circle treatment or badge styling.

## AMS-specific design rules

- Preserve the existing warm premium token system and stronger contrast.
- Favor one dominant accent per screen and reduce competing highlights.
- Use depth through layered shadows, dark hero backplates, and restrained gradients.
- Keep motion short and purposeful: reveal, active-state motion, press feedback.
- Prefer repeated surface patterns over one-off compositions.
- Design for RTL and LTR from the start.

## Practical translation

- Reference "home shortcuts" become AMS role-based quick actions.
- Reference "alerts feed" becomes AMS notifications and announcements flow.
- Reference "profile/about" becomes AMS account and settings summary cards.
- Reference "contact/service directory" becomes AMS vendors, managers, and resident help surfaces.
