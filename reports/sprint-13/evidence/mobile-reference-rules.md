# Mobile Reference Rules

## Source

- Reference asset reviewed: `mobile-app.mp4`
- Derived artifacts: `reports/sprint-13/evidence/reference-frames/mobile-reference-sheet.png` and `reports/sprint-13/evidence/reference-frames/frame-01.png` to `frame-08.png`

## Allowed To Borrow

- A four-destination mobile mental model with one clear active tab.
- A short home header that answers "what should I do next?" without a long hero.
- Single-column list rhythm with consistent card spacing and large tap targets.
- Repeated card families for feeds, settings groups, service categories, and profile summary panels.
- Compact grouped settings/profile sections instead of one long mixed form.
- Predictable accordion/list treatment for help, legal, or informational content.

## Forbidden To Copy

- The exact iconography, icon-in-circle styling, or card outlines from the reference.
- The reference palette, especially the pale pink and washed white treatment.
- Exact home action counts, ordering, copy, or Hebrew labels from the video.
- The reference photo/header treatment or any one-to-one screen composition.
- Any interaction detail that makes AMS look like a branded clone instead of its own product.

## AMS Direction

- Keep the existing warm premium palette and display typography.
- Increase contrast between page background, cards, muted rows, and active accents.
- Prefer one dominant accent per screen and reduce decorative competition.
- Use motion sparingly: short page-enter, subtle stagger, clear pressed states, calm active-tab movement.
- Favor thumb reach and first-screen comprehension over desktop-style density.

## Review Notes

- The reference is useful for clarity and information architecture, not for visual matching.
- AMS should feel denser in meaning but lighter in composition: fewer surfaces, cleaner grouping, stronger hierarchy.
- Any new mobile pattern should work in both RTL and LTR without depending on the reference layout.
